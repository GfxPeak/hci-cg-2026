Biography

Don Norman is a pioneer in the field of Human–Computer Interaction [cite: 98], known for his work on user-centered design, usability, and cognitive engineering[cite: 99].

Course Content & Importance

This site is based on Dix et al., Human–Computer Interaction (Chapters 9 and 12)[cite: 32, 42]. These chapters cover evaluation techniques and cognitive models that are fundamental to understanding how humans interact with technology.

Key Topics

  • Norman’s Action Cycle: Explains the psychology of interaction through seven stages, focusing on the "Gulfs" of Execution and Evaluation[cite: 42].
  • GOMS Framework: A predictive model that analyzes the Goals, Operators, Methods, and Selection rules an expert uses to perform tasks[cite: 42].
  • Keystroke-Level Model (KLM): A quantitative tool consisting of seven primitive operators (K, B, P, H, D, M, R) used to predict execution time for error-free interaction[cite: 123, 147].

Importance

Studying these models is critical because they allow designers to predict user performance and identify usability errors before a system is even built[cite: 108, 109]. While Norman helps us understand why errors happen, GOMS and KLM allow us to measure how efficiently a task can be completed[cite: 108, 132, 133].
